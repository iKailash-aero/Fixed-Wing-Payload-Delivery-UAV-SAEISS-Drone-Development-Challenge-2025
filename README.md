# Fixed-Wing Payload Delivery UAV – SAEISS Drone Development Challenge 2025

## Project Overview

This project focuses on the conceptual design, aerodynamic analysis, structural development, and fabrication planning of a fixed-wing UAV developed for payload delivery missions. The aircraft was designed to maximize payload carrying capability while maintaining aerodynamic efficiency, structural integrity, and stable flight characteristics.

## Objectives

* Design a fixed-wing UAV capable of carrying and delivering payloads
* Perform aircraft sizing based on mission requirements
* Select and evaluate suitable airfoils using XFLR5 analysis
* Develop wing, fuselage, empennage, and landing gear configurations
* Conduct CFD analysis for aerodynamic evaluation
* Perform structural design and optimization
* Select propulsion and electronic systems for mission requirements

## Software Used

* XFLR5
* ANSYS Fluent
* SolidWorks
* Microsoft Excel
* CAD Modelling Tools

## Methodology

1. Mission requirement analysis
2. Weight estimation and sizing calculations
3. Wing loading and aspect ratio determination
4. Airfoil analysis and selection
5. Wing design and structural development
6. Fuselage and payload mechanism design
7. Empennage sizing and stability considerations
8. CFD analysis for take-off and cruise conditions
9. Structural analysis and material evaluation
10. Optimization and component selection

## Aircraft Specifications

* Aircraft Type: Fixed-Wing UAV
* Maximum Take-Off Weight: 5 kg
* Payload Capacity: 0.5 kg
* Wingspan: 1.369 m
* Wing Area: 0.3125 m²
* Aspect Ratio: 6
* Fuselage Length: 1.026 m
* Propeller Diameter: 14 inch
* Propeller Pitch: 8.4 inch

## Airfoil Analysis

Multiple airfoils were evaluated using XFLR5:

* S9000
* NACA 4415
* NACA 3412
* NACA 2512
* MH114
* Clark Y
* CH10
* SD7080

Based on aerodynamic performance, lift characteristics, and stall behaviour, MH114 was selected as the most suitable airfoil for the mission profile.

## Wing Design

The aircraft utilizes:

* Mid-wing configuration
* Monoplane structure
* Rectangular wing planform
* Internal rib and spar arrangement for structural strength

The wing structure was optimized for ease of fabrication, adequate lift generation, and structural efficiency.

## Fuselage Design

A semi-monocoque fuselage structure was selected due to its balance between structural strength, aerodynamic efficiency, and payload integration capability.

The fuselage accommodates:

* Payload mechanism
* Electronics
* Propulsion system
* Control systems
* Landing gear

## CFD Analysis

Aerodynamic simulations were conducted using ANSYS Fluent to evaluate aircraft performance during take-off and cruise conditions.

### CFD Parameters

* Reynolds Number: 345,682
* Cruise Velocity: 24 m/s
* Air Density: 1.2256 kg/m³
* Turbulence Modelling
* Flow Visualization and Pressure Distribution Analysis

## Structural Analysis

Structural assessments were conducted to evaluate:

* Wing loading effects
* Fuselage strength
* Material performance
* Payload integration capability
* Aircraft stability under operational loads

## Materials Evaluated

* Balsa Wood
* Aluminium Square Hollow Sections

Comparative studies were performed to determine the optimal balance between strength and weight.

## Key Outcomes

* Successfully developed a complete fixed-wing UAV design for payload delivery missions
* Performed airfoil selection using aerodynamic analysis
* Designed and optimized wing and fuselage structures
* Validated aerodynamic characteristics through CFD analysis
* Achieved a lightweight and structurally efficient aircraft configuration
